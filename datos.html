FASHION, SPEED AND DIGITAL DESIRE

REF: OUTLOOP_RESEARCH_BRIEF_2026.pdf

1. FASHION WORKS AS IDENTITY
In digital culture, clothing is not only used: it is shown, posted and validated. Trends become part of self-expression, while platforms such as TikTok and Instagram accelerate the need to update personal image.

2. MICRO-TRENDS SHORTEN THE CYCLE
The research shows that young users move from seasonal fashion to visual trends that can last only weeks or days. Hauls, influencers and algorithmic content turn desire into a repeated buying habit.

3. THE IMPACT IS KNOWN BUT NOT ALWAYS FELT
Fast fashion is linked to high emissions, water use and textile waste. However, the interviews reveal a contradiction: many young consumers know the problem, but price, accessibility and social pressure still guide their purchases.

4. OUTLOOP TAKES A POSITION
This archive does not only report facts. It uses editorial design, collage, interaction and visual discomfort to translate sustainability into a language closer to digital natives: critical, experimental and impossible to scroll past.
